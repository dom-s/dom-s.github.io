About Me

Summary

Hi! I am a Ph.D. candidate in the computer science department at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign where I am part of the Text Information Management and Analysis Group. My advisor is Prof.Â ChengXiang Zhai.

My general research interests are at the intersection of Natural Language Processing, Information Retrieval, Machine Learning and Privacy/Security. My research focuses on modeling and understanding abusive user behavior on social media and underground forums, especially related to textual user data. To fight this abuse, I build ML systems that measure, monitor and mitigate illicit user activity. For example, I created the DarkJargon.net online resource for understanding underground communication with latent meaning.

As part of my research journey I have published work in text classification and retrieval, domain-adaptation, personalization, information extraction and knowledge bases.
